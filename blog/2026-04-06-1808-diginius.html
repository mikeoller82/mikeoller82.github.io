THE GOOGLE ANALYTICS PROBLEM

Google Analytics 4 is a mess. Here's why agencies are frustrated:

  • It's free, so support is non-existent: You get what you pay for. Google offers zero help when your data looks wrong.
  • Conversion tracking is broken by default: Setting up proper conversion events requires developer help. Diginius includes this out of the box.
  • It's designed for websites, not ad accounts: You cannot manage Google Ads from Google Analytics. You need a separate tool anyway.
  • Data sampling kicks in at 500k sessions: If you're running significant ad volume, GA4 starts estimating. Diginius gives you actual numbers.

The reality is that most agencies use Google Analytics as a traffic dashboard and then export to Excel for the client reporting that actually matters. Diginius replaces both steps with one platform.

WHERE DIGINIUS FALLS SHORT

No tool is perfect. Here's where Diginius doesn't compete:

  • Not for content publishers: If your primary metric is organic traffic and session duration, Google Analytics is still better for that.
  • UK-focused: Most documentation and support is in English and geared toward UK agencies. US agencies may find that limiting.
  • Not an enterprise tool: If you're managing $10M+ in ad spend, you probably need something more robust than Diginius.
  • Smaller community: There are far fewer tutorials and courses compared to Google Analytics. More self-learning required.
One G2 reviewer noted: "Diginius Insight is an all-in-one sales and marketing platform designed for both agencies and in-house teams. From a single, streamlined dashboard..." That's accurate. But it's designed for teams that need PPC focus, not general analytics.
