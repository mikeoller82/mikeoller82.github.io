THE "INSTANT" VIDEO CLAIM IS BULLSHIT

HeyGen's marketing suggests you can create a professional video in "minutes." Let's test that claim with a real-world scenario: creating a 2-minute product demo video.

Step 1: Scriptwriting (1-2 hours)
If you're a decent writer, you can crank out a script in about an hour. If you're not, you'll spend 3-4 hours wrestling with tone, pacing, and messaging. HeyGen doesn't write scripts for you—it just converts them to speech.

Step 2: Avatar Selection and Customization (30-60 minutes)
Browsing through hundreds of avatars, picking the right one, adjusting clothing, and setting up the perfect shot. Then you realize the avatar's lip movements don't match your script, so you spend another 30 minutes tweaking pronunciation.

Step 3: Scene Creation (1-2 hours)
Building out each scene, adding visuals, adjusting timing, making sure the avatar's gestures match the content. HeyGen's "auto-scene" feature creates generic backgrounds that look cheap and unprofessional.

Step 4: Audio Fine-Tuning (1-2 hours)
The AI voice sounds robotic out of the box. You'll spend hours adjusting pitch, speed, and emphasis to make it sound somewhat human. And don't get me started on the "emotional tone" settings—they're basically useless.

Total Time: 3.5 to 7 hours for a 2-minute video.

That's not "minutes." That's an entire workday for a 2-minute video. Compare that to hiring a freelance video editor on Fiverr who can deliver a professional video in 48 hours for $300, and suddenly HeyGen doesn't look like such a great deal.

THE UGLY TRUTH ABOUT DATA PRIVACY

Here's something HeyGen's marketing conveniently leaves out: they train their AI models on YOUR content. Every script you enter, every avatar you create, every video you produce—it all becomes training data for their algorithms.

Their terms of service (buried in Section 12) grant them a "worldwide, royalty-free, sublicensable, and transferable license to use, reproduce, modify, adapt, publish, translate, create derivative works from" your content.

Let that sink in. You're paying them to use their platform, and they're taking your intellectual property to train their AI. It's like paying someone to steal from you.

And don't think you can avoid this by using the "private mode." Their free plan doesn't even offer private hosting—everything you create is publicly accessible by default. Hope you weren't planning on making any proprietary content!
