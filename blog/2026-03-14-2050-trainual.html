What is Trainual?

Trainual is an AI-powered employee training and knowledge management platform designed to solve one of the most expensive problems in growing businesses: the knowledge that lives only in people's heads. When your best ops manager quits, when your senior developer leaves, when your front desk person moves on — they take years of institutional knowledge with them. Trainual captures that knowledge, structures it into reusable training content, and delivers it to the right people at the right time.

Founded in Scottsdale, Arizona, Trainual has grown to serve over 10,000 teams who have trained 1.25 million employees and documented 10.5 million processes on the platform. The core product sits at the intersection of three things: SOP documentation, employee onboarding, and role-based training paths. It's not trying to be an LMS for selling courses externally — it's focused entirely on internal training and operational consistency.

In 2026, Trainual has leaned heavily into AI-assisted content creation. Instead of spending hours writing training manuals from scratch, you describe what needs to be documented and Trainual's AI generates a structured, editable first draft. For businesses that know they should document everything but never have the time, this is the feature that changes the equation from "someday" to "done today."

Key Features

Trainual's feature set is tightly focused on internal operations and team enablement:

  • AI Content Generator: Describe a process in plain language and Trainual creates a structured training module with steps, descriptions, and formatting. Edit and refine the output instead of writing from a blank page. Reduces documentation time from hours to minutes.
  • SOP Builder: Create step-by-step standard operating procedures with rich media — images, videos, embedded documents, and screen recordings. Version control tracks changes, and employees always see the latest version. No more outdated PDFs floating around a shared drive.
  • Role-Based Training Paths: Assign specific training tracks to specific roles. New hires in different departments get different onboarding. The receptionist doesn't see the developer's deployment checklist. Automatic assignment based on role, department, or location.
  • Knowledge Base: A searchable, organized library of all your processes, policies, and procedures. Employees find answers without interrupting colleagues. Tagging, categorization, and full-text search make information discoverable instead of buried in a folder.
  • Tracking & Compliance: See who has completed which training, when they completed it, and how they scored on assessments. Automatic reminders for incomplete assignments. Completion reports for audits and compliance requirements. No more guessing whether someone actually read the safety manual.
  • Assessments & Quizzes: Test comprehension with built-in quizzes at the end of training modules. Multiple question types, passing score requirements, and automatic remediation — if someone fails, they're assigned the material again. Knowledge verification, not just checkbox completion.
  • Org Chart & People Directory: Visual org chart with reporting structures, roles, and responsibilities. Know who does what, who reports to whom, and what each person is responsible for. Links directly to relevant training for each position.
  • Integrations: Connects with HRIS systems, Slack, Gusto, BambooHR, Rippling, and other people-management tools. Auto-sync employee data so new hires are automatically onboarded and departing employees are offboarded without manual updates.

Who Is Trainual For?

Trainual's sweet spot is growing businesses that have moved past the "everyone knows everything" phase and entered the "we need systems or we'll collapse" phase:

Growing small businesses (10-200 employees) that are hiring faster than they can onboard. If new employees take weeks to become productive because they're learning through osmosis and interrupting colleagues, Trainual provides structured, self-service onboarding that gets people productive in days. The owner or ops manager who has been personally training every new hire can finally delegate that responsibility to documented systems.

Franchises and multi-location businesses that need operational consistency across locations. When every location does things slightly differently, quality suffers and customers notice. Trainual ensures the same processes, standards, and expectations are documented and trained at every location. Update a procedure once and every location gets the update automatically.

Remote and hybrid teams that can't rely on hallway conversations and shoulder-tap learning. Documentation is the only way knowledge transfers in distributed teams. Trainual gives remote employees the same depth of institutional knowledge that office teams absorb through proximity — without scheduling another Zoom call.

Professional services firms (agencies, consultancies, accounting firms) where process consistency directly affects client outcomes. When every team member follows the same methodology, deliverables are predictable and quality is consistent. Trainual ensures methodology isn't just understood by senior staff — it's trained into every new hire from day one.

The most expensive employee isn't the one with the highest salary. It's the one who leaves and takes all their knowledge with them. Trainual makes sure that never happens again.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • AI content generator turns blank pages into structured training modules in minutes
  • Role-based training paths ensure the right people get the right content — no information overload
  • Completion tracking and compliance reporting satisfy audit requirements without manual spreadsheets
  • Intuitive interface that non-technical managers can use without training on the training tool
  • Searchable knowledge base reduces "how do I do this?" interruptions across the organization
  • Org chart with linked responsibilities creates clarity about who does what
  • Regular product updates and responsive customer support team

Cons

  • Pricing is per-user, which gets expensive for large teams (100+ employees)
  • External course selling is not supported — internal training only, no B2C functionality
  • AI-generated content requires editing — it's a strong first draft, not a final product
  • Mobile experience is functional but not as polished as the desktop interface
  • Advanced HRIS integrations are limited compared to enterprise HCM platforms

Pricing

Trainual offers three pricing tiers based on team size and feature needs:

  • Small Business — $250/month: Up to 50 people. Full SOP builder, AI content generator, training paths, knowledge base, basic analytics, and email support. The most popular plan for growing businesses that need to professionalize their onboarding and documentation.
  • Growth — $400/month: Up to 100 people. Everything in Small Business plus advanced analytics, custom branding, API access, and priority support. For businesses scaling quickly and needing deeper insights into training effectiveness.
  • Enterprise — Custom pricing: Unlimited people. Everything in Growth plus dedicated success manager, custom integrations, SSO, advanced compliance features, and SLA guarantees. For larger organizations with complex training and compliance requirements.

How Trainual Compares

Against Notion and Confluence, Trainual is purpose-built for training and onboarding rather than general knowledge management. Notion is more flexible; Trainual is more structured. If you want a wiki, use Notion. If you want training paths with completion tracking, assessments, and compliance reporting, Trainual is the right tool.

Against LMS platforms like TalentLMS or Docebo, Trainual is simpler and more focused on operations rather than corporate learning and development. Enterprise LMS platforms handle complex learning management with certifications, competency frameworks, and SCORM compliance. Trainual handles "here's how we do things, learn it, confirm you learned it" — and does it with far less complexity.

Against Google Docs and shared drives, there's no comparison on structure and accountability. Google Docs are flexible but invisible — nobody knows what exists, what's current, or whether anyone read it. Trainual makes documentation active, assigned, tracked, and verified.
