The 30-Day Support Ticket Autopsy

We run a small e-commerce store — about 800 orders per month, two customer support reps, and a support inbox that averages 40-60 tickets per day. Before Tidio, the workflow was standard: customer sends a message through the website chat widget, email, or social media. It lands in a shared inbox. A rep reads it, categorizes it, looks up the order in Shopify, and responds. Average response time: 47 minutes during business hours. After hours? Messages sat until morning.

We installed Tidio with Lyro AI enabled and gave it access to our FAQ database, shipping policy, return policy, and Shopify order data. For 30 days, we let Lyro handle every incoming conversation first. If it couldn't resolve the issue, it escalated to a human rep. We tracked resolution rates, customer satisfaction scores, and the actual workload reduction on our human team.

The results were better than expected — and I went in skeptical. Lyro resolved 64% of all incoming conversations without human intervention. Not "deflected to an article." Resolved. The customer got their answer, confirmed it was helpful, and the conversation ended. Our average response time dropped from 47 minutes to under 2 minutes — because AI handles it instantly and humans only get the complex cases that actually need judgment.

Every single one required a rep to open Shopify, look up the order, copy the tracking number, and write a response. Tidio's Lyro AI handles these automatically — it pulls the order status from Shopify and tells the customer exactly where their package is. We measured 92% resolution rate on order status inquiries. That's not an approximation. That's our actual number from the 30-day test.

Questions like "does this come in blue?" or "what's the return window?" were answered accurately. More complex questions — "will this work with my specific setup?" — got escalated to humans, which is exactly the right behavior.

The after-hours coverage alone justified the cost. Before Tidio, we estimated we were losing 8-12 potential customers per night to unanswered chat messages. With Lyro running 24/7, those conversations happen at 11 PM just as well as at 2 PM. We can't measure exactly how many sales this recovered, but our weekend revenue increased 18% in the month after implementation — a strong signal that after-hours support matters.

What Tidio Doesn't Tell You

Being honest about where the AI falls short:

  • Training takes real effort. Lyro's 64% resolution rate didn't happen out of the box. We spent about 6 hours feeding it FAQs, correcting wrong answers, and tuning responses. Without that investment, the initial resolution rate was closer to 40%. The tool works, but it's not magic — it needs your institutional knowledge to perform.
  • Edge cases still need humans. Emotional customers, complex multi-issue complaints, and unusual situations consistently went sideways with AI. The escalation to human reps handled these fine, but if your business has a lot of complex support scenarios, expect the AI resolution rate to be lower than 64%.
  • The free tier is limited. 50 Lyro AI conversations per month on the free plan. That's roughly one day's worth of tickets for a modest business. Serious use requires the paid Lyro AI plan.
Our support reps didn't lose their jobs — they got their time back. Instead of answering "where's my order?" 40 times a day, they're now handling complex issues that actually require human judgment. That's a better use of everyone's time.
