The Stack That Was Bleeding Us Dry

Our outbound sales stack had three problems: it was expensive, it was fragmented, and nobody on the team actually liked using it. We had ZoomInfo for prospecting ($15K/year), Outreach for sequencing ($100/user/month), and HubSpot as our CRM ($50/user/month on Starter). That's roughly $600-800 per rep per month before a single email got sent.

Worse, the data lived in three places. A rep would find a contact in ZoomInfo, manually enter it into HubSpot, then copy the email address into Outreach to build a sequence. Two hours of onboarding per new hire just to teach them where things lived. The data got stale between systems because nothing synced cleanly without buying the expensive integration tiers.

A buddy of mine who runs a 12-person SDR team told me to look at Apollo.io. "It does all three," he said. I was skeptical — tools that claim to do everything usually do nothing well. But the math on a free tier was worth a test.

What Apollo.io Actually Is

Apollo is a sales intelligence and engagement platform that bundles four traditionally separate tools into one interface:

  • Contact database — 210 million+ verified B2B contacts with filtering by industry, company size, seniority, technographics, and funding data
  • Email sequencing — Multi-step automated outreach with A/B testing and inbox warmup
  • Built-in CRM — Pipeline management, activity tracking, and deal stages without leaving the platform
  • Dialer — Cloud-based calling with recording and conversation intelligence (US dialer on Professional, international on Organization)

The Chrome extension plugs directly into LinkedIn. You visit a profile, Apollo pulls the contact data, and one click saves it to a sequence. No copy-pasting. No switching tabs.

On paper, this replaces three to four separate subscriptions. In practice? It mostly delivers — with some caveats I'll get into.

The Data: Best Database for the Price

Apollo's contact database is the core product, and it's genuinely impressive for the cost. We tested against our existing ZoomInfo data on a list of 500 known-good contacts from our CRM. Here's what we found:

  • Email accuracy: Roughly 70-80% of email addresses matched what we had on file. That's not ZoomInfo-level (which claims 90%+), but ZoomInfo costs 20x more.
  • Phone accuracy: Weaker. Mobile numbers were correct about 50-60% of the time, and they cost more credits to pull. For cold calling, budget extra and expect some duds.
  • Job title freshness: Mixed. Apollo tracks job changes, but we still found contacts who had moved companies 3-6 months ago with stale data. Better than some competitors, worse than LinkedIn Sales Navigator.

The advanced filters are where Apollo genuinely shines. You can build hyper-targeted lists by combining industry vertical, employee count, technology stack, recent funding rounds, and seniority level. We built a list of "Series B SaaS companies with 50-200 employees using HubSpot" in under two minutes. That used to take a data analyst half a day with ZoomInfo's clunky interface.

Sequencing: Good Enough for Most Teams

The email sequencing engine handles the basics well. Multi-step sequences with conditional branching, automatic follow-ups, A/B subject line testing, and reply detection all work as expected. The inbox warmup feature is a nice touch — it gradually ramps sending volume from a new mailbox to avoid spam filters.

Where it falls short compared to dedicated tools like Outreach or Salesloft:

  • Multichannel gaps. Apollo supports email, phone, and LinkedIn tasks in sequences, but the LinkedIn and phone steps are mostly manual. You get a reminder to send a connection request or make a call — it doesn't do it automatically. True multichannel automation requires more manual oversight.
  • Reporting depth. The analytics cover open rates, reply rates, and sequence performance. But if you need detailed attribution modeling, team leaderboard comparisons, or A/B test confidence intervals, you'll find the reporting thin compared to enterprise tools.
  • Deliverability management. Apollo offers inbox rotation and warmup, which is good. But dedicated deliverability platforms like Instantly or Warmbox give you more granular control over reputation tracking and blacklist monitoring.

For a team running standard outbound email campaigns — cold outreach, nurture sequences, follow-up drips — Apollo's sequencing is more than capable. For complex multi-touch, multi-channel campaigns with deep reporting, you might still want a specialist tool.

The CRM That Replaces HubSpot Starter

This was the surprise. I expected Apollo's CRM to be an afterthought — a checkbox feature that technically exists but nobody actually uses. It's better than that.

The pipeline view is clean. Deal stages are customizable. Activity tracking logs emails, calls, and tasks automatically. Team collaboration features let managers see rep activity without asking for reports. For a team that was using HubSpot Starter primarily as a deal tracker and contact database, Apollo's CRM handles the job without friction.

Integration with Salesforce and HubSpot exists for teams that want to keep their primary CRM and use Apollo as a data/enrichment layer. We tested the HubSpot sync and it worked — contacts and activity flowed bidirectionally without manual intervention.

If you're running complex sales processes with custom objects, multi-currency support, or advanced workflow automation, you'll outgrow Apollo's CRM fast. But for SMB teams running straightforward pipeline management, it's genuinely functional.

Pricing: The Elephant in the Room

Apollo's pricing is both its biggest selling point and its most confusing aspect. The headline prices look great:

  • Free: $0 — 50 AI credits, basic contact access, 2 sequences. Enough to test, not enough to run real outbound.
  • Basic: $49/user/month (annual) — Unlimited email credits, 75 mobile credits, 1,000 exports/month, advanced filters, CRM integrations.
  • Professional: $79/user/month (annual) — 100 mobile credits, 2,000 exports, A/B testing, US dialer, call recording. This is the sweet spot for most teams.
  • Organization: $119/user/month (annual, minimum 3 users) — International dialer, custom reports, API access, dedicated CSM.

Here's the catch: credits. Apollo uses a hybrid model where certain actions consume credits, and credits expire monthly. Revealing a mobile phone number costs significantly more credits than pulling an email. Heavy phone outreach can burn through your monthly allocation fast.

Real-world cost for a team doing aggressive outbound: budget $150-400 per user per month depending on how much phone data you need. That's still a fraction of ZoomInfo's pricing, but it's 2-3x the advertised rate. Plan accordingly.

What We Actually Saved

After 90 days on Apollo Professional, here's the real comparison against our previous stack:

  • Old stack: ZoomInfo ($1,250/mo) + Outreach ($100/user × 3 = $300/mo) + HubSpot Starter ($50/user × 3 = $150/mo) = $1,700/month
  • Apollo: Professional plan ($79/user × 3 = $237/mo) + credit overages (~$80/mo) = ~$317/month

Monthly savings: ~$1,383. Annual savings: ~$16,600. We redirected about half of that into better ad spend and a part-time SDR hire.

The tradeoff is data quality on phone numbers (worse than ZoomInfo) and less powerful sequencing (worse than Outreach for complex campaigns). For our use case — mostly email-first outbound with occasional calls — those tradeoffs were worth it.

Who Should Use Apollo (and Who Shouldn't)

Apollo is a strong fit if you:

  • Run email-heavy outbound campaigns and need good-enough data at a fraction of enterprise pricing
  • Want to consolidate your sales stack into one platform instead of juggling three tools
  • Have a small to mid-size team (2-20 reps) and don't need enterprise-grade reporting
  • Are currently paying for ZoomInfo and want to test a cheaper alternative
  • Need a functional CRM without paying HubSpot or Salesforce prices

Apollo might disappoint if you:

  • Depend on highly accurate mobile phone data for cold calling at scale
  • Need true end-to-end multichannel automation (email + LinkedIn + phone all automated)
  • Run complex sales processes requiring custom CRM objects and advanced workflow automation
  • Have a team of 50+ reps needing granular permission controls and enterprise security compliance

The Bottom Line

Apollo.io isn't the best tool at any single job. ZoomInfo has better data. Outreach has better sequencing. Salesforce has a better CRM. But Apollo is good enough at all three to replace them for most SMB and mid-market sales teams — at a price that makes the math obvious.

The free tier exists to get you hooked. It works. Start there, run a real test with your team's workflow, and upgrade when the credit limits force your hand. That's what we did, and we never looked back at the old stack.

If you're paying for more than one sales tool and your team is under 20 reps, you probably only need Apollo. Test it before renewing anything else.
