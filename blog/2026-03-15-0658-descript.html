What is Descript?

Descript is an AI-powered video and audio editing platform built on a radical premise: what if editing video worked like editing a Google Doc? Instead of scrubbing through timelines, juggling tracks, and wrestling with keyframes, you work from an AI-generated transcript. Delete a sentence from the text, and that moment vanishes from the video. Rearrange paragraphs to restructure your content. Copy and paste sections between projects. It's disorientingly intuitive — and it works.

Backed by OpenAI and launched in 2017 as a transcription tool, Descript has evolved into a full production suite. In 2026, it includes AI-powered video editing, podcast production, screen recording, voice cloning (Overdub), automatic filler word removal, green screen replacement, eye contact correction, and an AI editing assistant called Underlord that proactively suggests cuts and improvements. The platform handles the entire workflow from recording to publishing.

What makes Descript genuinely different from Final Cut, Premiere, or even CapCut is the audience it serves. This isn't a tool for professional video editors — it's a tool for everyone else. Marketers, educators, podcasters, YouTubers, sales teams, and internal communications professionals who need polished video but have never learned (and don't want to learn) traditional editing. The learning curve is measured in minutes, not weeks.

Key Features

Descript's feature set reflects its mission to make video editing accessible to non-editors:

  • Transcript-Based Editing: Record or import your video, and Descript transcribes it automatically. Edit the transcript — cut words, rearrange sentences, delete filler — and the video follows. This is Descript's core innovation, and it changes everything about the editing experience.
  • Underlord AI Editor: An AI assistant that watches your footage and suggests edits. Automatically removes "um," "uh," and "like" in one click. Cuts dead air and long pauses. Identifies repeated takes and recommends the best one. It's like having an editor who pre-screens your footage before you touch it.
  • Overdub Voice Cloning: Record a voice sample and Descript can generate new audio in your voice from text input. Fix a mispronounced name by typing the correction. Add a missing sentence without re-recording. The cloned voice handles short corrections convincingly — longer passages are less reliable.
  • Studio Sound: One-click audio enhancement that strips background noise, reduces echo, and enhances voice clarity. The results are startling — a recording from a laptop mic in a coffee shop can sound close to studio quality after processing. No audio engineering knowledge required.
  • Eye Contact Correction: AI adjusts your gaze so it appears you're looking directly at the camera — even if you were reading from a script or notes. A small feature that makes a significant difference in how professional your on-camera presence feels.
  • Social Clip Generator: AI identifies the most engaging moments from long-form content and automatically creates short clips for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Adds captions, converts aspect ratios, and formats for each platform. One long video becomes a week of social content in minutes.
  • Collaborative Editing: Google Docs-style real-time collaboration. Multiple team members edit simultaneously, leave comments, suggest changes, and track version history. No more passing files back and forth or merging conflicting edits.
  • Direct Publishing: Export to YouTube, podcast hosts, social platforms, or download in any format. Templates for common content types speed up the final polish. One-click publishing eliminates the export-upload-describe cycle.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Transcript-based editing is genuinely transformative — saves hours per video for talking-head content
  • Underlord AI handles tedious cleanup (fillers, dead air, repeated takes) in seconds
  • Studio Sound produces remarkable audio quality from mediocre source recordings
  • Social clip generator automates content repurposing across platforms
  • Eye contact correction adds professionalism without re-recording
  • Collaboration features rival Google Docs for team editing workflows
  • Free tier is genuinely usable for evaluating the full workflow

Cons

  • Fine-grained timeline editing is less precise than Premiere Pro or DaVinci for complex projects
  • Transcription accuracy drops with heavy accents, jargon, or poor audio quality
  • Rendering and export times can be slow on longer videos with AI features enabled
  • Overdub works for short corrections but sounds unnatural for extended generated passages
  • Most features require an internet connection — limited offline functionality
