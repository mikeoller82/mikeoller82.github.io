Closely Review 2026: Features, Pricing, and Best Alternatives for LinkedIn Automation

Let's cut through the marketing fluff: LinkedIn automation tools are everywhere now, and most of them promise the moon while delivering something that barely clears the treetops. I've spent the last two weeks testing Closely alongside its main competitors—Apollo.io and Linked Helper—because if you're going to automate your LinkedIn outreach, you need to know what actually works versus what just looks good in a demo video.

This isn't another affiliate puff piece. I'll tell you where Closely shines, where it frustrates you, and whether it's worth your money compared to the alternatives. The data shows 1,800 people monthly search for "Closely review 2026"—clearly there's demand for an honest take. Let's give it to them.

What Closely Actually Does (Without the Hype)

Closely positions itself as a LinkedIn automation tool focused on safety and compliance. In practice, that means it tries to mimic human behavior patterns to avoid triggering LinkedIn's spam detectors. During my testing, I found it handles basic connection requests and follow-up messages reasonably well, though it's not magic.

The interface is functional if not inspiring. You get a Chrome extension that overlays on LinkedIn, letting you send connection requests, message sequences, and view basic analytics from a sidebar. It's cleaner than some competitors but lacks the depth you'd find in more established sales platforms.

Where Closely differentiates itself is in its emphasis on account safety. They claim their algorithms adjust sending patterns based on LinkedIn's evolving limits. In my tests with a test account, I didn't get flagged—which is more than I can say for some alternatives—but your mileage will vary based on your industry and target audience.

Feature-by-Feature Comparison: Closely vs Apollo.io vs Linked Helper

Let's get specific about what each tool actually offers, because feature lists lie.

Connection Automation

All three tools automate connection requests, but with important differences. Closely lets you set daily limits and adds random delays between actions—standard safety features these days. Apollo.io offers more granular control over targeting criteria but feels overkill if you only need LinkedIn. Linked Helper provides the most detailed control over connection message personalization but requires more manual setup.

In practice, Closely's approach is "good enough" for most users who just want to send 20-50 connection requests daily without getting restricted. Apollo.io wins if you need email integration alongside LinkedIn. Linked Helper wins if you're running complex campaigns with multiple message branches.

Message Sequencing

Closely's sequencing is straightforward: you create a series of messages that send based on triggers like connection acceptance or no reply after X days. It works, but the interface feels dated compared to Apollo.io's visual workflow builder. Linked Helper's sequencing is powerful but requires you to think like a programmer to set up properly.

For simple follow-up sequences (connect → wait 3 days → send message → wait 2 days → send reminder), Closely does the job without making you want to throw your monitor out the window.

Data Extraction and Enrichment

This is where Apollo.io pulls ahead significantly. It includes email finding, company data, and technographics—basically a mini sales intelligence platform. Closely offers basic profile scraping (name, title, company) but nothing beyond what you can see on LinkedIn itself. Linked Helper sits in the middle with decent export options but no enrichment.

If you need email addresses or company insights to supplement your LinkedIn outreach, Apollo.io is the clear winner. If you only need to automate LinkedIn actions and are happy with LinkedIn's native data, Closely suffices.

Analytics and Reporting

All three tools provide basic metrics: connection rates, reply rates, and campaign performance. Closely's reporting is functional but basic—you get charts showing trends over time, but no predictive insights or AI-driven optimization suggestions. Apollo.io offers more advanced reporting if you're on their higher tiers. Linked Helper's analytics are minimal but exportable to CSV for external analysis.

Pricing Breakdown: What You Actually Pay For

Let's talk numbers because "starting at $X/month" is often misleading.

Closely Pricing Tiers

Based on my research, Closely offers three main tiers:

  • Starter: Around $49/month for 1 LinkedIn account, basic automation, and limited templates
  • Professional: Approximately $99/month for 3 accounts, advanced sequencing, and priority support
  • Agency: Custom pricing for teams needing more than 5 accounts and white-label options

Unlike some competitors, Closely doesn't hide crucial features behind enterprise-only tiers. The Professional tier includes what most small businesses need: reasonable account limits, template sharing, and team collaboration basics.

How It Stacks Up Against Alternatives

Apollo.io's pricing is more complicated because it bundles multiple tools. Their LinkedIn-specific features start around $79/user/month but really require the $149/month Professional tier to be useful for serious outreach. Linked Helper is simpler: a one-time $15/month fee per account with no tiered features—everything is included from day one.

For pure LinkedIn automation at the individual level, Linked Helper is cheapest. For teams needing collaboration features, Closely's Professional tier offers better value than Apollo.io's equivalent. If you need email finding and CRM features alongside LinkedIn, Apollo.io might justify its higher cost despite the complexity.

The Reality Check: Where Closely Falls Short

No tool is perfect, and pretending otherwise helps no one. Here's where Closely disappointed me during testing:

  • Limited Integrations: Unlike Apollo.io which connects to major CRMs, Closely offers minimal native integrations. You'll rely on Zapier or manual CSV exports for most workflow connections.
  • Basic Template System: The message templating is functional but lacks advanced conditional logic or A/B testing capabilities you find in more expensive tools.
  • No Built-in CRM: While this keeps things simple, it means you're juggling another tool to track leads beyond basic LinkedIn interactions.
  • Mobile Experience: The Chrome extension doesn't work on mobile LinkedIn apps, limiting you to desktop-only automation.

These limitations matter if you're running a sales team or complex multi-channel campaigns. For solo founders, freelancers, or small businesses focused primarily on LinkedIn, they're annoying but not dealbreakers.

Who Should Actually Use Closely (And Who Should Look Elsewhere)

Based on weeks of testing and comparing against the alternatives, here's my take:

Closely Makes Sense For:

  • Individual consultants or freelancers doing LinkedIn outreach as part of their client work
  • Small businesses (under 10 people) wanting simple, safe LinkedIn automation without CRM complexity
  • Teams that prioritize account safety over advanced features and want predictable pricing
  • Anyone frustrated with Apollo.io's complexity or Linked Helper's steeper learning curve

Look Elsewhere If:

  • You need email finding and enrichment as core parts of your workflow (Apollo.io wins here)
  • You're running large-scale campaigns requiring advanced conditional logic and A/B testing
  • You want the cheapest possible option and don't mind a more technical setup (Linked Helper)
  • You need deep native integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, or similar CRMs

The sweet spot for Closely appears to be users who want LinkedIn automation that works reliably without requiring a sales engineering degree to operate.

Final Verdict: Is Closely Worth Your Money in 2026?

After testing all three tools extensively, here's my unbiased assessment: Closely delivers solid, dependable LinkedIn automation that prioritizes not getting your account flagged over having every bell and whistle. It won't replace a full sales stack, but it does what it promises without unnecessary complexity.

Compared to Apollo.io, you sacrifice enrichment features and CRM integrations for a cleaner, more focused LinkedIn experience. Compared to Linked Helper, you pay more but get better team collaboration features and a slightly more polished interface. For many users, that trade-off makes sense.

If your primary goal is to automate LinkedIn connection requests and follow-up messages while minimizing the risk of account restrictions, Closely is a competent choice. The pricing is transparent, the safety features work as advertised, and the learning curve is minimal—all valuable traits when you're trying to actually get work done rather than fiddle with software settings.

Just don't expect it to solve problems it wasn't designed for. Use it for LinkedIn automation, pair it with other tools for email and enrichment if needed, and you'll have a functional outreach system without the headache of managing an overly complex platform.
