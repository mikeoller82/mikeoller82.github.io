WHAT THE ACTUAL WORKFLOW LOOKS LIKE NOW

Here's the before and after of what Kit changed for me. This is the real value, not the marketing pitch.

Before: The Manual Grind

Every morning I would:

  1. Open my email tool and filter subscribers by signup date
  2. Identify who was at day 3, day 5, day 7, or day 14 of my sequence
  3. Copy-paste the appropriate email template for each stage
  4. Personalize the first line with each person's name
  5. Hit send for each one individually
  6. Mark in my spreadsheet who I'd emailed that day so I didn't double-email anyone
  7. Repeat tomorrow

That's about 25-30 minutes per day, five days a week, for just over two hours every week. In a month, that's roughly 10 hours of my life spent on something a machine should be doing. And the moment I missed a day or got busy, the entire sequence fell apart. Prospects who'd signed up on Monday would get their follow-up on Thursday instead of Wednesday, and the timing that I'd carefully calculated in my head went out the window.

After: The Kit Automation

With Kit, I set this up once:

  1. Create a visual automation for "New Subscriber Welcome Sequence"
  2. Set trigger: "Subscriber joins tag [Free Guide]"
  3. Add delay: 2 minutes → send email "Here's your download"
  4. Add delay: 2 days → send email "3 tips to get more from [topic]"
  5. Add delay: 5 days → send email "Here's what I recommend next"
  6. Add delay: 7 days → send email "Last chance - this offer ends"
  7. Save and activate

Now when someone signs up, the sequence runs automatically. Day 1, day 3, day 5, day 7 — it's all pre-programmed. I haven't manually sent a sequence email in eight months. The automation just runs. Every new subscriber gets the exact same carefully crafted experience, timed perfectly, without me touching anything.

THE SPECIFIC FEATURES THAT MAKE THIS WORK

Kit isn't the most feature-rich email tool on the market. It doesn't have everything MailerLite or Klaviyo offers. But the features it does have are specifically designed for this exact automation use case, and they work.

The visual automation builder is the star of the show. You don't need to know anything about coding or technical setup. You drag a trigger onto the canvas, connect it to an action (send email), add a delay, connect another action, and you're done. It's built for people who want automation without needing a degree in marketing technology. One reviewer on Dreamgrow put it accurately: "It's stupidly easy to build automated sequences. Anyone can create a powerful sequence that hits your audience with the right offer at the right time."

The tag-based system is the other piece that makes this work. When someone signs up, you can automatically tag them based on what they downloaded, what page they came from, or what action they took. Then your automation triggers based on that tag. It's simple but powerful. You don't need complex conditional logic — just "if tag equals X, run sequence Y."

Landing pages are included, which means you don't need a separate tool for capturing email addresses. The templates aren't the most beautiful I've ever seen, but they're functional and they convert. You can have a lead capture page live in about 15 minutes without touching any code.

THE COMPLAINTS YOU SHOULD KNOW ABOUT

No tool is perfect and Kit has real tradeoffs. Here's what actual users are complaining about:

The email editor is basic. One Sender.net reviewer wrote: "I've hit performance issues too — slow loading times and glitches in the email editor that interrupt my flow when I'm trying to get work done." This matches my experience. The editor works but it's not as smooth as what you'd get in Mailchimp or Klaviyo. If you're someone who spends hours designing beautiful emails, you'll get frustrated. If you're someone who just wants the email to send and convert, it's fine.

It's not cheap as you scale. The free plan handles up to 300 subscribers, which is great for testing. But once you pass 1,000 subscribers, you're looking at $39/month on the Creator plan, and it goes up from there. For a business making money from email, that's reasonable. For someone just starting out, it can feel expensive fast.

The deliverability is good but not perfect. Some users report that Kit emails land in the promotions tab more often than other platforms. It's not a dealbreaker but worth knowing if you're trying to land in the primary inbox.

One user on Reddit in r/Newsletters noted: "I don't know if this is the right sub for this, but basically, my client and I are using ConvertKit. He wants me to add a button that will redirect to an external link after someone subscribes. This seems like it should be simple but it took forever to figure out."

The small things sometimes take longer than they should. A simple redirect that would be a checkbox in another tool required digging through documentation in Kit.
