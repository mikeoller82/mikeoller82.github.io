The Keyboard Is the Bottleneck

The average person types 45 words per minute. They think at roughly 400 words per minute. That gap — the 8x difference between how fast you think and how fast you can get ideas onto a screen — is the single biggest productivity bottleneck in knowledge work.

Voice-to-text technology has promised to close this gap for decades. Dragon NaturallySpeaking launched in 1997. Google Voice Typing arrived in 2012. Apple added dictation to every device. And yet, almost nobody uses them daily. Why? Because they all produce raw, unedited transcription — stream-of-consciousness dumps that require as much editing time as typing would have taken in the first place.

Wispr Flow is the first voice-to-text tool that solves this fundamental problem. It doesn't just transcribe what you say — it polishes it. Rambling thoughts become clear sentences. Filler words disappear. Formatting appears automatically. The result isn't a transcription; it's a finished piece of writing.

What Is Wispr Flow?

Flow is a system-wide voice dictation tool that works in every application on your device. On Mac and Windows, it replaces your keyboard's dictation mode with something dramatically smarter. On iPhone and Android, it becomes your default voice input across all apps.

The core promise: speak naturally and get polished, formatted text instantly. Not a rough transcript you need to clean up — actual writing that reads like you typed it, but produced at 220 words per minute instead of 45.

The secret is Flow's AI Auto Edit engine. As you speak, the AI processes your words in real-time, removing filler words ("um," "uh," "you know"), fixing grammar, adding punctuation, applying formatting, and adjusting tone based on the app you're using. An email sounds professional. A Slack message sounds casual. A legal document sounds precise. All from the same speech input.
