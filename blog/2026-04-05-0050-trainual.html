THE BRUTAL TRUTH ABOUT TRAINUAL

Here's the honest truth about Trainual: if you're a company that doesn't document anything, don't bother. If your processes live in someone's head and not in writing, Trainual won't fix that — it just makes the mess more accessible. If you're looking for a magic tool that will document your disorganized company for you, look elsewhere.

Trainual is built for companies that already document or are committed to starting. It's a documentation delivery system, not a documentation creation system. The platform makes it easy to create training materials, track completion, and verify understanding — but you still need to write the content.

But here's the other truth: for the right company, Trainual does something no other tool does — it makes training measurable. You can see exactly who completed what, when they completed it, and how they scored on verification. That visibility is worth gold for growing organizations.

WHO SHOULD ABSOLUTELY NOT USE TRAINUAL

Let me be direct about who wastes their time with Trainual:

  • Companies that don't document — If you've never written procedures, Trainual won't magically create them. You're buying a delivery mechanism without content. Write first, then automate.
  • One-person teams — At $25/seat/month with 10-seat minimum, Trainual costs $250/month minimum. For a solopreneur, that's overkill.
  • Companies that resist documentation — If your team pushes back on written procedures, buying Trainual won't change that. The tool is for people who want to document.
  • Budget-first buyers — Notion and Google Docs are free. If price is your only concern, Trainual isn't for you.

If any of those describe you, save your money. Trainual will feel like an expensive shelf for content you never create.

WHO TRAINUAL ACTUALLY DELIVERS FOR

Now here's who Trainual was built for:

Growing companies with 10-100 employees. Where processes need to scale without the founder explaining everything personally. One G2 reviewer noted: "I was spending 3 hours daily answering same questions. Now I spend maybe 20 minutes updating content." That's the value.

Companies with turnover. Restaurants, franchises, agencies — places that hire frequently. Trainual cuts onboarding time from weeks to days because new hires self-service through the knowledge base.

Compliance-heavy industries. If you need to prove people were trained on specific procedures, the completion tracking and quiz features are exactly what you need. One Capterra review noted: "It offers strong control over training paths and compliance."

The Self-Serve Plan at $25/seat/month with 10-seat minimum ($250/month total) makes sense for organizations that would otherwise spend significantly more on onboarding and re-training.

THE REAL PROBLEMS WORTH KNOWING

No tool is perfect. Here's what actual users complain about:

The mobile experience isn't great. A review noted: "buyers should weigh trade-offs like limited mobile polish." If your team does training on phones constantly, you'll notice the mobile interface is more limited than desktop.

Adding seats gets expensive fast. 10 seats = $250/month. 30 seats = $750/month. Above 50, you're in Enterprise pricing territory. The costs add up significantly at scale.

Content creation still takes time. As one Reddit user put it: "The challenge with Trainual is sometimes try to be everything to everyone, which drives up costs without necessarily adding value." You're paying for features some teams won't use.

The tool doesn't replace the need to write. Trainual organizes and delivers documentation, but someone still needs to author it. If that person doesn't exist in your organization, Trainual won't create them.

G2 sums it up: "Users note that Trainual is not the cheapest solution, but they believe it's worth the investment for quality support." Translation: it's pricey, the tool is decent, and you pay for the support team.

THE VERDICT

Trainual is not for everyone. It's not even for most companies. If you don't document, don't have the budget, or don't have someone committed to creating content — save your money. The tool will sit empty.

But if you're a growing company that already documents or is committed to starting, that needs to onboard faster, and that needs visibility into training completion — Trainual delivers. The $250/month minimum for 10 seats is significant, but so is the time saved on repeated training.

The math is simple. If Trainual saves you 10 hours/week in重复 training, at $50/hour that's $500/week in recovered time. The $250/month cost pays for itself quickly for the right organization.

This is a tool for companies ready to systematize their knowledge. Not everyone is. That's okay. But if you are, Trainual might be exactly what you've been looking for.
