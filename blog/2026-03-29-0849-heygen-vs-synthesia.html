The Feature Breakdown That Actually Matters

Forget feature lists pulled from marketing pages. Here's what actually separates these two platforms when you sit down and start making videos.

Avatar Realism: Where HeyGen Pulls Ahead

HeyGen's Avatar IV technology is the reason this comparison exists at all. These aren't the stiff, obviously-AI avatars from 2024. Avatar IV uses motion capture-based animation with natural eye movement, fluid hand gestures, and micro-expressions that register as human. I tested both platforms with the same script and showed the output to five people — all five identified HeyGen's avatar as "real" before being told it was AI-generated.

Synthesia's Expressive Avatars are competent. They have accurate lip sync, natural blinking, and they look professional in a corporate sense. But there's a polish ceiling with Synthesia that HeyGen has broken through. Synthesia avatars look like really good stock photos that move. HeyGen avatars look like people.

The Digital Twins feature pushes this further. Upload a photo and a voice sample, and HeyGen builds an avatar that looks and sounds like you. For founders, coaches, and personal brands — this is the feature that makes the subscription pay for itself in the first week. Synthesia doesn't offer anything equivalent.

Language Support: 175 vs 140

HeyGen supports 175+ languages and dialects. Synthesia supports 140+. On paper, both numbers seem excessive until you're working with a company that sells into Southeast Asia, Africa, or the Middle East and needs content in Tagalog, Swahili, or Urdu. HeyGen wins the coverage map.

More importantly, HeyGen's real-time translation lets you generate a video in English and automatically create versions in 30+ languages with lip sync intact. You don't re-record. You don't re-edit. You hit translate and get 30 publish-ready videos. Synthesia offers translation too, but HeyGen's implementation is faster and the lip sync quality holds up better across languages.

Video Editing: Synthesia's One Clear Win

Here's where Synthesia earns its keep. The timeline-based editing, scene management, and granular control over pacing and transitions is genuinely more powerful than HeyGen's editor. If you're producing multi-scene training modules with branded intros, overlays, quizzes, and chapter markers — Synthesia's editor was built for that workflow.

HeyGen's editor is simpler and more intuitive. For most creators, that's actually better. You paste a script, pick an avatar, choose a background, and publish. The friction is near-zero. But if you need pixel-perfect scene transitions and complex interactive elements, Synthesia gives you more control.

Pricing: Where the Real Difference Lives

This is the section that actually decides which platform you pick. Features matter, but budget determines everything.

HeyGen Pricing

HeyGen uses a straightforward plan model with unlimited video creation on paid tiers:

  • Free: $0 — limited minutes, watermarked, test the platform
  • Creator: $24/month (annual) or $29/month (monthly) — unlimited videos, 1 seat
  • Team: $30/seat/month (annual) or $39/seat/month (monthly) — 2 seat minimum, collaboration features
  • Business: $72/month — advanced features, priority rendering
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing — SOC 2 compliance, dedicated support

The Creator plan at $24/month with unlimited video creation is the deal here. For a solo creator or small marketing team, that's hard to beat.

Synthesia Pricing

Synthesia uses a subscription model with video minute caps:

  • Free: $0 — 3 minutes/month, limited avatars
  • Starter: $18/month (billed annually) — 180 minutes/year, 1 seat, 90+ avatars
  • Creator: $59/month (billed annually) — 360 minutes/year, 1 seat
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing — unlimited avatars, SOC 2 Type II certified, full compliance

Synthesia's Starter plan at $18/month looks cheaper until you do the math. 180 minutes per year is 15 minutes per month. If you're producing even a modest amount of content, you'll burn through that in a week. HeyGen's unlimited creation on the $24 plan means you never think about minute caps again.

The Verdict

For most people reading this — creators, marketers, small business owners, solopreneurs — HeyGen wins. Better avatars, more languages, unlimited creation at $24/month, and the Digital Twins feature that Synthesia can't match. The AI video space moves fast. Both platforms ship updates monthly. But as of March 2026, HeyGen has the edge on what matters most: quality, flexibility, and value for money.
