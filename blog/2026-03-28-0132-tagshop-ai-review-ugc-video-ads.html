Where It Actually Falls Short

I'm not going to pretend this tool has no issues. Here's what to watch for:

  • Script quality varies wildly. Some auto-generated scripts are genuinely sharp. Others read like a robot learned marketing from a 2019 blog. You need to review and edit every script — don't just generate and publish.
  • Custom avatar cloning takes practice. The digital clone feature (upload a video of yourself, get a reusable avatar) works, but the quality depends heavily on your source footage. Good lighting and a clean background matter a lot.
  • It's built for direct response. If you need brand storytelling, emotional narratives, or cinematic quality — this isn't your tool. It's designed for "here's the product, here's why you need it, buy now" style ads.
  • Some avatars still feel slightly off. While the library is impressive, not every avatar nails natural movement. You'll want to preview a few options before committing.
