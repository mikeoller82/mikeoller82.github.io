Where Carepatron Will Disappoint You

I'm not going to pretend this is a perfect platform. Here are the genuine weaknesses:

  • AI note-taking is basic — the transcription works, but multiple testers report it's "just okay." Don't expect it to write your SOAP notes perfectly. You'll still need to edit. It saves maybe 30% of documentation time, not 80%.
  • Limited reporting — if you need detailed analytics on client outcomes, session frequency trends, or revenue forecasting, Carepatron doesn't have it. Growing practices that rely on data-driven decisions will feel this gap.
  • No multi-location support — if you run practices in two or three cities, Carepatron wasn't built for that. One location, one account.
  • Insurance billing can get expensive — the per-claim charges add up. If your practice is 80%+ insurance-based, factor this into the real cost.
  • No inventory management — not relevant for most therapists, but if you sell supplements, skincare, or products, you'll need a separate system.
