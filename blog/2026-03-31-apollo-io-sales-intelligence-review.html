What Apollo Actually Does (Without the Marketing Fluff)

Apollo is not just a contact database. It is a full sales engagement platform crammed into one interface. Here is what you actually get:

✅ CORE FEATURES

  • 265M+ contact database with email, phone, company data, technographics, and intent signals
  • Built-in email sequences — multi-step outreach with A/B testing, no third-party tool needed
  • Chrome extension — pull contacts from LinkedIn, company websites, and Gmail in one click
  • AI lead scoring — machine learning ranks prospects by conversion likelihood
  • Dialer with call recording — cold call directly from the platform with automatic logging
  • Buyer intent data — see which companies are actively searching for your solution

❌ HONEST DRAWBACKS

  • Data accuracy averages 65% — drops to 60-73% outside the US depending on region
  • Email bounce rates of 15-25% — higher than dedicated verification tools
  • Mobile number accuracy around 67% — improved but still not reliable for cold calling
  • Free tier is limited — 50 AI credits, basic search, 2 sequences max
  • UI can feel overwhelming — too many features in one dashboard

The data accuracy issue is real, and anyone telling you otherwise is selling you something. Apollo's 265 million contacts sound impressive, but a meaningful chunk of those emails will bounce. The workaround is simple: run Apollo exports through a verification tool like NeverBounce or ZeroBounce before launching campaigns. Annoying? Yes. Worth it for the volume Apollo gives you? Also yes.

Who Should (And Should Not) Use Apollo

Apollo is not for everyone. Here is the honest breakdown:

Perfect fit if you:

  • Run outbound sales with a team of 2-50 SDRs
  • Need a single tool instead of paying for ZoomInfo + Outreach + a dialer
  • Target US-based B2B companies (data quality is strongest there)
  • Want to start free and scale into paid as revenue grows
  • Are comfortable verifying email lists before sending

Skip it if you:

  • Need 95%+ email accuracy out of the box (try Lusha or Cognism instead)
  • Primarily target non-US markets (data gaps are significant)
  • Already have a deep ZoomInfo contract you cannot break
  • Sell to consumers — Apollo is B2B only
