Where WhatConverts Completely Fails

I'm not going to sugarcoat this. There's a real problem that multiple users have raised, and it's worth knowing before you commit.

WhatConverts counts repeat contacts as separate leads. If a prospect calls once, books an appointment, then calls back four times about logistics — that's five leads in WhatConverts' eyes. To any marketer with a brain, that's one lead with five touch points.

This inflates your lead counts and makes your cost-per-lead numbers look better than reality. One Reddit user on r/PPC put it bluntly:

"WhatConverts' default reports show ALL leads — including repeat leads... But WhatConverts support tells me that it's not possible to filter out repeat leads in reports — EVEN CUSTOM REPORTS! Any marketer should be reporting on unique leads, not touch points."

This is a genuine limitation. The quotable lead feature partially mitigates it (you can mark repeat contacts as non-quotable), but it requires manual work. If automated unique lead deduplication is a dealbreaker for your reporting, you'll need to work around this.

There's also a learning curve with the dashboard. It's powerful, but the initial setup — connecting all lead sources, configuring tracking numbers, training clients to mark leads — takes real effort. Plan for a week of setup per client, not an afternoon.
