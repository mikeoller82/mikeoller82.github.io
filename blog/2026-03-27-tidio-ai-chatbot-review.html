What Tidio Actually Is (And Isn't)

Tidio is an AI-powered customer service chatbot built for small businesses. It's not a generic "type your question" widget. It uses Lyro AI — a conversational engine that actually understands context, remembers previous messages, and can resolve up to 70% of customer inquiries without human intervention.

What it does well:

  • Answers FAQs using your knowledge base (you upload docs, it learns)
  • Handles order tracking, returns, and product questions
  • Books appointments and demos directly in the chat
  • Escalates to a human when it hits its limits (no hallucinating answers)
  • Works across your website, Instagram DMs, and Facebook Messenger

What it doesn't do:

  • Replace a full customer support team for complex issues
  • Handle highly technical or regulated industry questions well
  • Magic — if your knowledge base is empty, the bot has nothing to work with

The Setup: Easier Than I Expected

I'm not exaggerating when I say the install took about 5 minutes. You add a snippet of JavaScript to your site (or use their WordPress/Shopify plugin), and the chat widget appears. That's it for the basic setup.

The real work — and this is important — is building your knowledge base. Tidio's Lyro AI learns from:

  • FAQ pages you point it to
  • Help center articles
  • Documents you upload (PDFs, docs)
  • Previous conversation history

I spent about 30 minutes uploading my FAQ content and product descriptions. That's when the bot went from "generic assistant" to "actually useful."

Who Should (And Shouldn't) Use Tidio

Great fit if you:

  • Run an e-commerce store (Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce)
  • Get 100+ website visitors per day but low conversion
  • Sell services and need to qualify leads after hours
  • Don't have a support team but get repetitive questions

Probably not for you if:

  • Your product is highly technical and needs expert-level support
  • You get fewer than 20 visitors per day (not enough data for the AI)
  • You need enterprise-grade compliance (HIPAA, SOC2)
