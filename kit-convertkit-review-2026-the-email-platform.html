Email Marketing That Feels Like Writing to Friends

Kit's email editor is designed for people who write—literally. The clean, distraction-free interface feels like composing a letter, not building a marketing campaign. Write text-first emails that actually get read because they feel personal, not templated.

But behind that simplicity sits serious power. Visual automations let you build complex subscriber journeys: welcome sequences, product launch funnels, re-engagement campaigns, and behavioral triggers. Tag subscribers based on actions—what they clicked, what they bought, which forms they filled out—and deliver perfectly timed follow-ups.

A/B testing covers subject lines, send times, and content variations. Deliverability rates are industry-leading because Kit maintains strict sender reputation standards. Your emails land in inboxes, not spam folders. That alone is worth the switch from platforms that let anyone send anything.

Creator Commerce: Sell Digital Products Without a Separate Store

Kit's built-in commerce platform lets you sell digital products, paid newsletters, coaching sessions, and memberships directly through your email platform. No need for Gumroad, Teachable, or Patreon—Kit handles the product pages, checkout, delivery, and post-purchase follow-up.

Products sync directly with your email lists and automations. When someone purchases, they're automatically tagged, added to post-purchase sequences, and granted access to exclusive content. The entire sales funnel lives inside Kit, from first touch to final delivery.

The commerce system supports one-time payments, subscriptions, and pay-what-you-want pricing. Transaction fees are competitive, and the checkout experience is smooth and mobile-optimized. For creators selling digital products, this eliminates the need for a separate e-commerce platform entirely.

Newsletter Growth Through Recommendations

Kit's recommendation engine is a growth multiplier. When subscribers finish reading your email, they see recommendations for other newsletters in the network—and your newsletter gets recommended to their subscribers too. It's cross-promotion that runs on autopilot.

The creator network means your newsletter surfaces to engaged, email-reading audiences who have already opted in. Unlike social media followers who might never see your posts, recommended subscribers actively chose to hear from you. These tend to be higher-quality subscribers with better engagement rates.

Kit also supports paid recommendations—earn money when subscribers join other newsletters through your recommendations. This turns your thank-you page and confirmation emails into revenue streams, even before you're selling your own products.

Landing Pages & Sign-Up Forms

Kit's landing page builder offers dozens of clean, conversion-optimized templates. Create squeeze pages, lead magnet delivery pages, webinar registration forms, and waitlists—all connected directly to your email sequences and automations.

Forms support embedded, pop-up, slide-in, and full-page formats. Every form feeds directly into Kit's tagging and automation system, so new subscribers immediately enter the right sequence based on which form they used. No manual list management, no CSV uploads, no integration headaches.

The visual builder is intentionally simple—creators spend time creating, not learning design software. Customize colors, fonts, and layout, then publish. Pages are hosted on Kit's infrastructure with fast load times and mobile optimization.

Visual Automations Without the Complexity

Kit's automation builder maps out subscriber journeys visually. See exactly what happens at every step: new subscriber → welcome email → tag based on interest → wait 3 days → send educational sequence → pitch product → follow up if no purchase. The entire flow is visible, editable, and testable.

Automations support branching logic, time delays, conditional splits based on tags or actions, and event triggers from external tools via integrations. Connect to Zapier, Make, or Kit's API to trigger sequences from purchases, webinar attendance, course completions, or any custom event.

The power-to-simplicity ratio is where Kit stands out. Advanced marketers get the behavioral triggers and conditional logic they need. Beginners get a guided experience that makes complex automations feel straightforward. Neither group compromises.

Kit vs. The Competition

vs. Mailchimp: Kit is built for creators; Mailchimp is built for businesses. Kit's tag-based system is more flexible than Mailchimp's list-based approach, and the automation builder is more intuitive. Mailchimp offers broader e-commerce integrations but charges more as you scale.

vs. Beehiiv: Beehiiv excels at newsletter growth and ad monetization. Kit offers stronger email automation and built-in commerce for selling products. Choose Beehiiv if your primary model is ad-supported newsletters; choose Kit if you're selling products, courses, or coaching alongside your newsletter.

vs. ActiveCampaign: ActiveCampaign offers deeper CRM and sales automation features for B2B. Kit is simpler, more creator-focused, and includes commerce out of the box. For most creators, Kit covers everything ActiveCampaign does with a fraction of the complexity.

vs. Substack: Substack locks you into their platform and takes 10% of paid subscriptions. Kit gives you ownership, better automation, and lower fees. The tradeoff is Kit requires slightly more setup—Substack is simpler to start but more limiting to scale.
